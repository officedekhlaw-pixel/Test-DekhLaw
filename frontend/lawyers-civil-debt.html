Understanding Personal & Family Law in India

Family law in India is governed by multiple personal laws based on religion and the secular Special Marriage Act. Here's what you need to know.

Divorce Law in India

Divorce in India is governed by different laws: the Hindu Marriage Act 1955 (for Hindus, Sikhs, Buddhists, Jains), the Special Marriage Act 1954, the Muslim Personal Law, and the Indian Divorce Act 1869 (for Christians). A mutual consent divorce under Section 13B is usually faster (6–18 months). A contested divorce can take 2–5 years depending on the court and complexity.

Child Custody Rights

Indian courts award custody under the Guardians and Wards Act 1890 and relevant personal laws, always prioritising the best interest of the child. Courts may award joint custody, sole custody or define visitation rights. If you are seeking or defending a custody order, legal representation significantly impacts the outcome.

Domestic Violence & Protection

The Protection of Women from Domestic Violence Act 2005 provides civil remedies including Protection Orders, Residence Orders, and Monetary Relief. Filing a DV application does not require you to file a criminal complaint first. A family lawyer can help you draft and file the Domestic Incident Report and obtain urgent relief from the magistrate.

Muslim Personal Law

Under Muslim Personal Law (Shariat) Application Act 1937, matters of marriage (Nikah), divorce (Talaq, Khula, Mubarat), maintenance (Mehr), and inheritance are governed separately. The Supreme Court's 2017 judgment in Shayara Bano declared instant Triple Talaq unconstitutional. Our Muslim law specialists can advise on MPLB, PMLA and family court proceedings.

Medical Negligence Claims

Medical negligence cases in India can be filed before the Consumer Forum under the Consumer Protection Act 2019, or as a civil suit for damages, or as a criminal complaint under Section 304A IPC. Establishing negligence requires expert medical evidence. Our lawyers work with medical consultants to build strong cases for compensation.

Motor Accident & MACT

Victims of motor accidents can claim compensation through the Motor Accident Claims Tribunal (MACT) under the Motor Vehicles Act 1988. Compensation covers medical expenses, loss of income, pain and suffering, and in fatal cases, dependency benefits for the family. Timely filing (within 6 months is ideal) and proper documentation are crucial.
